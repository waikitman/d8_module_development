Bricklayers Insurance Essentials

Find an insurance adviser

Bricklayers, as with many other tradies, are faced with a variety of hazards on the job. With the day to day tasks and the daily running of a small business, it can be hard to find the time to sit down and consider your risks and insurance needs.

Bricklayers risks

By understanding what risks you face, you’ll be able to better understand what insurance policies are most necessary for your particular bricklaying business.

Bricklayers insurance needs

Anything can happen at any time whilst you’re on site. However with insurance you can take away the worry and begin to work confidently knowing that your business is protected.

Risks for bricklayers

We recognise that running a small business can be complex. That is why we’ve partnered with a network of insurance advisers who can help assess your risks and suggest what cover will best suit your unique needs.

Speak to your local adviser today

Top risks for bricklayers:

Product Liability

Protect your business from damages that occur from injury or property damage that results from your businesses Bricklaying, this includes things that your business builds, sells, or supplies.

More details

Public Liability

Protect your business from damage that may occur while you are on site. This includes damage caused to the businesses or people around you.

More details

Workers Compensation

Bricklaying can be a hazardous industry, due to the changes locations and exposure to the elements. You can feel confident that in the event of injury or sickness to your staff that they will be looked after and you can continue to run your business.

More details

Other important risks for bricklayers:

Motor Vehicle Risks

As a Bricklayer your car is vital to your work, with Motor Vehicle cover you can be confident that the vehicles you work with, and the tools stored inside them with are covered in the event of an accident.

More details

Material Damage

Having your building material stolen, damaged or go missing, can significantly compromise your commitments. Make sure that you can continue running your business by protecting yourself with Material Damage cover.

More details

Machinery and Engineering

If a machine like your Mixer breaks down and stops working you need to feel confident that you can still continue running your business. Machinery and engineering cover protects you from breakdown of the machinery that you use to do your job.

More details

Marine Transit

Materials are important to Bricklaying but damage and loss can happen even before they are on site. Ensure that the materials you need to do your job are covered during their transportation.

Product Liability

Bricklayers, as with many other tradies, are faced with a variety of hazards on the job. With the day to day tasks and the daily running of a small business, it can be hard to find the time to sit down and consider your risks and insurance needs.

Top product liability risks

  • Faulty workmanship – This may lead to damage of property or injury to third parties long after you’ve finished the job
  • The use of low quality products and materials – Once again, this can lead to injuries or damage to property later on
  • Not adhering to building codes and specifications – Be careful to check these carefully before beginning a project, as non-compliance can leave you open for legal action
  • Property damage – Your workmanship can lead to damage of your clients’ property
